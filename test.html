trips and travels



Rajasthan is a state in Northwest India. It comprises the inhospitable Thar Desert. There are several major tourist cities in Rajasthan, in this blog we will cover our experience in 2 of the major tourist cities, Jaisulmer and Jodhpur. Our journy starts in the the city of Jaisulmer, which is also called the Golden City. Jaisulmer is home to desirable yellow sandstone which is mined and transported around the world. Most building that have been built for centuries have been built with this sandstone, when the sun sets the city lights up a golden color. Like most cities in Rajasthan, Jaisulmer's landscape is definded by a magnificent fort that has protected the city for centruries. The fort is unique because people still live in the fort to this day, even a King(who doesn't rule over anything). Rajasthan is also known for its wonderful archatectural buildings and antique doors(like the one photographed above). These are not ruins by any means, they are still functional and are still part of everyday life.

Places to visit in Jaisulmer:

Patwaon Havali:
Rajasthan is home to hundreds if not thousands of Havalies which are like royal mansions; they involve intrecate carvings in what looks like wood but is actually carved yellow sandstone.




Jaisalmer Fort
It is one of the few "living forts" in the world. Some portion of the old city's polpulation still resides inside of the fort. The yellow sandstone helps camouflage the fort in the yellow desert. As you make your way through the fort, you can't help but be in awe of the majestic walls with their intricate stone carvings. Inside you will find resedences, museums, temples and bazars.



Bada Bagh
Bada Bagh is a royal cenotaph. It is a picturesque monument that is best viewed during the early morning(sunrise) or the late evening(sunset).




Gadisar Lake
Gadisar Lake is an artificial lake in Jaisalmer. It was built by king Jaisal in 1156 AD and later rebuilt around 1367 AD. It is said that this lake once provided water to the entire city. You will find this place to be immensly crowded in the evenings however if you are an early bird, you will almost have the entire place to yourselves.

Food

Food is an important part of any travel, so make time in your itinerary to sample the local delicacies. Breakfast here starts heavy (you're going to need this for all the walking you're about to do), Parathas lathered with ghee is a staple. Donâ€™t forget to try Ker Sangri for lunch. It is a curry using ingredients that grows on trees and shrubs* that are found in abundance in the desert. This curry goes well with Bajra Roti. Try eating with your hands like the locals do,as it adds to the taste. Most lodging places have a terrace restaurant, make sure you arrive early so you can get a table with a great view of the forts and the city! Another must-try in Rajasthan is Kachori*, which is a deep-fried crispy bread snack with various savory fillings, followed by lassi, which is a buttermilk drink that is a quencher in the desert heat. Make sure to stop by and try some street food, the locals can help you spot the good ones. We tried Shahi Samosa which was by the clock tower in the blue city(Jodhpur). The food justified the standing room only crowd in the store. Chai*(NOT Chai Tea, as that is redundant) is the quintessential drink if you are in Northern India, you will find many local roadside Chai shops that serve you the Chai in biodegradable terracotta cups which are great for the environment.











Transportation

No matter where you land in India, you can fly to Jodhpur, Rajasthan. I would recomend flying IndiGo which is one of the leading domestic and internation airlines that India offers. When you get to Jodhpur you can hire a traveler minivan for the entirety of your trip. The driver is also your guide and it helps because they are local to the area. All the major cities in Rajasthan are 5 hours away from Jodhpur. If you can, try and stay awake for the ride because you can see some amazing scenery along the way. The scenery ranges from wild camels munching on trees to women and men dressed in local attire, traffic-navigating cows crossing the road. With 2-wheelers, bikes, lorries (trucks), and 4-wheelers all sharing the same road, the lanes become fluid and the cars move around in zig-zags so it's best to sit back and let the experts do the driving, they make it quite comfortable.Inside the city,you can also wave down an auto-rickshaw(auto) which is a very common 3-wheeled vehicle. Riding in the back of one of these vehicles is fun! The experience is one of the best I have had! It does not matter how you get around just enjoy the travel as it will take a long time and you get to see a lot of senery that you would not get to see if you were flying to different places.

Desert Camping/Glamping

Desert camping (or more like glamping), is an experience you will never forget. From the jeep rides to the freezing cold showers, sand everywhere, camel rides ,the memories will stay with you forever. There are many companies that offer desert glamping where you can spend the night in tents, and wake up to camels and peacocks. There is usually a dinner program with authentic Rajasthani food and dance performances for all the guests. This can be an adventure packed day with dune bashing, camel back riding, parasailing and Jeep safaris. The Thar desert is considered one of the best spots for stargazing. Try to plan your trip around no moon day so you can get a glimpse of the Milky Way! The concept of desert glamping caters to those who seek adventure but prefer not to compromise on comfort. It attracts a diverse range of travelers, from nature enthusiasts looking to immerse themselves in the unique desert ecosystem to luxury seekers who want a taste of the high life against a backdrop of untouched wilderness.
