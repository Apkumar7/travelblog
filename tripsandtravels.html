Rajasthan is a state in Northwest India. It comprises the inhospitable Thar Desert. There are several major tourist cities in Rajasthan. In this blog, I will cover two of the major tourist cities, Jaisalmer and Jodhpur. Our journey starts in the the city of Jaisalmer, which is also called the Golden City. Jaisalmer is home to desirable yellow sandstone which is mined and exported around the world. Most buildings in the city are built with this sandstone. When the sun sets, the city lights up a golden color. Like most cities in Rajasthan, Jaisalmer's landscape is defined by a magnificent fort that has protected the city for centuries. The fort is unique because people still live in the fort to this day, even the royal family. Rajasthan is also known for its wonderful architectural buildings and antique doors(like the one photographed above). These are not ruins by any means, they are still functional and are part of everyday life.

Jaisalmer

Places to visit in Jaisalmer

Patwaon ki Haveli:

Rajasthan is home to hundreds if not thousands of Havelies which are royal mansions; they involve intricate carvings in what looks like wood but is actually carved yellow sandstone.




Jaisalmer Fort

Jaisalmer Fort is one of the few "living forts" in the world. Some portion of the old city's population still resides inside of the fort. The yellow sandstone helps camouflage the fort in the yellow desert. As you make your way through the fort, you can't help but be in awe of the majestic walls with some intricate stone carvings. Inside, you will find residences, museums, temples and bazaars.



Bada Bagh

Bada Bagh is a royal cenotaph. It is a picturesque monument that is best viewed during sunrise or the sunset.




Gadisar lake

Gadisar Lake is an artificial lake in Jaisalmer. It was built by King Jaisal in 1156 AD and later rebuilt around 1367 AD. It is said that this lake once provided water to the entire city. You will find the lake to be immensly crowded in the evenings however if you are an early bird, you will almost have the entire place to yourself.

Rajkumari Ratnavati Girls School

You might not see this listed on most travel blogs, but it is a must see in my opinion. It is very close to the desert camping resorts, so you might pass right by it if you are not looking for it. Historically, girl's education has been a difficult subject in India and a lot is being done to improve access to education. Rajkumari Ratnavathi Girls school is a school in rural Thar Desert of Jaisalmer. Its unique architecture allows it to withstand the extreme heat of the desert. The oval-shaped structure is built with golden sandstone and it is effectively angled to allow wind to circulate cool air. The building is designed in such a way that use of air conditioning is not required!

Jodhpur

Jodhpur is a city in the Thar Desert of Rajasthan. Jodhpur is a popular tourist destination. It is about a 5 hour drive by road from Jaisalmer. It features palaces, forts and temples. It is known as the Blue City. On the way, you will get a glimpse of rural life in India. Don't forget to stop by local tea shops on the way, Kesar (saffron) Chai is a specialty here. Served in terracotta cups, it is best enjoyed sitting on the cots that are laid out in the front of such tea shops.

Places to visit in Jodhpur

Mehrangarh Fort

Mehrangarh Fort stands a hundred feet on a perpendicular cliff, defining the majestic skyline of Jodhpur. It is one of the best preserved forts in India. Rudyard Kipling called it "the work of giants" due to its colossal proportions. It has been known that elephants were once used to break into the fort during wars. There is an elevator, but if possible, it is recommended to walk up the fort to experience this architectural wonder. Inside the fort, you will also see palaces and museums. These feature collections of Arms, Armours, textiles, furniture, and the famous miniature paintings of Rajasthan. Once you reach the top of the fort, you will be rewarded with a beautiful view of the Blue City below.

Jaswanth Thada

Jaswanth Thada is a cenotaph located on the way to the Mehrangarh fort. It is also the "Taj Mahal" of Marwar. Built with Makrana marble featuring intricately carved designs, it is an architectural wonder. It's best visited during the day when the sun illuminates the transparent marble.

Walk Through the Blue City

Jodhpur is known as the blue city as buildings in the oldest district are painted in shades of blue. Although many explanations exist for the origins of the custom of painting homes blue, it is a sight to behold and now defines the city of Jodhpur as a contrast to the desert shades of the arid Thar Desert. You can catch a glimpse of the Blue City from the fort and other viewpoints across the city, walking through the narrow lanes lined with homes painted in shades of blue embellished with murals depicting traditional rural life. There are several spots along the way that provide a great backdrop for photography, remember to wear shades of blue! As the narrow lanes can be winding, it is best to go with a guide so you don't lose your way but it is not a bad place to get lost.

Ghanta Ghar (Clock Tower)

The clock tower is a prominent landmark and represents the major shopping area of Jodhpur. It also has cultural significance, and is the heart of Jodhpur. Surrounding the clock tower is the lively Sardar Market. Get ready for crowds and the buzzling streets lined with bazaars selling Juthis(traditional leather footwear), textiles with traditional Rajasthani block prints, spices and famous local eateries featuring street food. Bargaining is common practice so don't hesitate to negotiate prices with shopkeepers respectfully.

Mandore Garden

Once a royal monument, now converted to a park, it still hosts the centuries old architectural heritage of temples, monuments and fort. As you enter the gardens, you will be welcomed by a barrel of Gray Langurs running amok in the garden. Be sure to keep your eatables secure. As you stroll through the gardens, you will be surrounded by stunning architecture and intricate carvings. Locals walking and relaxing leisurely in the garden with monkeys running around seemingly oblivious to the centuries old architectural masterpieces, is an experience in and of itself.

Toorji Ka Jhalra (Stepwell)

Toorji stepwell is a stunning architectural marvel from the 18th century. The stepwell was constructed to provide the locals a reliable source of freshwater. Even though it was originally built as a water source, it is now a major tourist attraction where you can witness an amazing feat of ancient engineering and artistry.

Food

Food is an important part of any travel, so make time in your itinerary to sample the local delicacies.
Breakfast here starts heavy (you're going to need this for all the walking you're about to do), Parathas lathered with ghee is a staple. Don’t forget to try Ker Sangri for lunch. It is a curry using ingredients that grows on trees and shrubs* that are found in abundance in the desert. This curry goes well with Bajra Roti. Try eating with your hands like the locals do,as it adds to the taste.
Most lodging places have a terrace restaurant, make sure you arrive early so you can get a table with a great view of the forts and the city!
Another must-try in Rajasthan is Kachori*, which is a deep-fried crispy bread snack with various savory fillings, followed by lassi, which is a buttermilk drink that is a quencher in the desert heat. Make sure to stop by and try some street food, the locals can help you spot the good ones. We tried Shahi Samosa which was by the clock tower in the blue city(Jodhpur). The food justified the standing room only crowd in the store.
Chai*(NOT Chai Tea, as that is redundant) is the quintessential drink if you are in Northern India. You will find many local roadside Chai shops that serve you Chai in biodegradable terracotta cups which are great for the environment.

Transportation

No matter where you land in India, you can fly to Jodhpur, Rajasthan. I would recommend flying IndiGo which is one of the leading domestic and international airlines in India.
When you get to Jodhpur you can hire a traveler minivan for the entirety of your trip. The driver is also your guide and it helps because they are local to the area. All the major cities in Rajasthan are 5 hours away from Jodhpur. If you can, try and stay awake for the ride because you can see some amazing scenery along the way.
The scenery ranges from wild camels munching on trees to women and men dressed in local attire to traffic-navigating cows crossing the road. With 2-wheelers, bikes, lorries (trucks), and 4-wheelers all sharing the same road, the lanes become fluid and the cars move around in zig-zags so it's best to sit back and let the experts do the driving- they make it quite comfortable. Inside the city, you can also wave down an auto-rickshaw(auto) which is a very common 3-wheeled vehicle. Riding in the back of one of these vehicles is fun! The experience is one of the best I have had!
It does not matter how you get around, just enjoy the journey as you will get to see scenes that you would miss if you were flying.

Desert Camping

Desert camping (or glamping), is an experience you will never forget. From the jeep rides to the freezing cold showers, sand everywhere, camel rides, the memories will stay with you forever.
There are many companies that offer desert glamping where you can spend the night in tents, and wake up to camels and peacocks. There is usually a dinner program with authentic Rajasthani food and dance performances for all the guests.
This can be an adventure packed day with dune bashing, camel back riding, parasailing and Jeep safaris.
The Thar desert is considered one of the best spots for stargazing. Try to plan your trip around no moon day so you can get a glimpse of the Milky Way!
The concept of desert glamping caters to those who seek adventure but prefer not to compromise on comfort. It attracts a diverse range of travelers, from nature enthusiasts looking to immerse themselves in the unique desert ecosystem to luxury seekers who want a taste of the high life against a backdrop of untouched wilderness.
